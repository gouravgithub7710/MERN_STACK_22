Kallam Anji Reddy (10 August 1941 – 15 March 2013) was an Indian entrepreneur in the pharmaceutical industry, the founder-chairman of Dr. Reddy's Laboratories,, which he established in 1984, and chairman of Dr Reddy's Foundation (DRF), corporate social responsibility arm of the group, established 1996. [1] [2] He was a member of the Indian Prime Minister's Council on Trade and Industry.[3] [4]

Life

Reddy was born in Padala, a village in Kurnool district of Andhra Pradesh, India, to a family of farmers. He graduated in science from Nizam College, Hyderabad, and obtained a Bachelor of Chemical Engineering degree from Osmania University in 1964. He later obtained a master's degree in chemical engineering from the University of Michigan, United States, in 1966. After working for a brief period at Vicks Inc., he returned to India in 1968 and joined Indian Drugs and Pharmaceuticals Limited (IDPL) , a government-owned pharmaceutical manufacturer, as a production engineer. In 1973, he left IDPL to join Nectar Life Sciences as its general manager. In 1984, he founded Dr. Reddy's Laboratories along with Nallamothu Bhaskar Rao, P.V. Rama Rao, and K. Satyanarayana by borrowing ₹2.5 lakh from his wife.

Reddy died on 15 March 2013 at the Apollo Hospital in Hyderabad, India, after a brief illness. He was 71 years old. He is survived by his wife, Kallam Anji Reddy, and their two children, Kallam Satish Reddy and Kallam Venkata Prasad.

Awarded

Reddy was awarded the Padma Bhushan, India's third-highest civilian award, in 2001 for his contributions to the Indian pharmaceutical industry. He was also a recipient of the Ernst & Young Entrepreneur of the Year Award in 2003.
